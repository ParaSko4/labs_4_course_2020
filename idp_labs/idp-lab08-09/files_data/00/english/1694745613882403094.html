Time For Lunch! These Easy Recipes Are Good For Work, School, and Other Busy Weekdays

18 Nov 2019, 00:35 by Lauren Harano

If you're on the lookout for healthy, hearty, and satisfying lunches to bring to work, we rounded up the easiest recipes out there. From veggie-filled wraps to protein-heavy salads, these lunches will keep your cravings at bay and fuel you for anything that comes your way — inside and outside the office. Whether you prefer to meal prep the week before or like to whip it up the morning of, these lunchtime recipes are about to breathe new life into your workweek routine. Check out tasty salads, wholesome sandwiches, and filling soups, ahead!

— Additional reporting by Lisette Mejia and Lizzie Fuhr

1 Sweet Potato, Chickpea, and Quinoa Burger

This sweet potato, chickpea, and quinoa veggie burger is perfectly spiced and completely satisfying. At 202 calories per patty, you can add a 120-calorie whole-wheat bun and lettuce, tomato, and ketchup (34 calories) and still come in at well under 500 calories. Make multiple patties in advance to enjoy as a sandwich or on top of greens for the week.

2 Italian Zucchini Pasta Spring Rolls

These Italian zucchini pasta spring rolls are a fresh and healthy take on an Italian hero sandwich.

3 Hemp and Cabbage Detox Salad

If you're on the hunt for a quick and tasty detoxifying recipe, this crunchy cabbage and hemp salad is for you. If you haven't had them yet, it's time to give hemp seeds a try. They're a great source of omega-3s and vitamin E for vegans and vegetarians, and the combination of the cold-pressed oil and seeds in this recipe gives this fresh salad a nutty flavor you'll love.

4 Farro Salad With Citrus Vinaigrette

A hearty farro salad is guaranteed to give you the energy needed for a long day at work. The citrus vinaigrette dressing and fruit add a lively spring to this salad's step.

5 Carrot Pasta

This carrot Paleo pasta is made with carrot ribbons using a simple vegetable peeler, then sautéed with mushrooms, red peppers, cherry tomatoes, and fresh basil for a bowl that's sure to satisfy your pasta cravings.

6 Quinoa Broccoli Slaw With Honey-Mustard Dressing

Broccoli slaw transforms from side dish to full-fledged meal when quinoa is added to the mix.

7 Kale and Wild Rice Casserole

Gruyère, mushrooms, and caramelized onions add umami oomph to a kale and wild rice casserole. Reheat a leftover slice for a lovely lunch.

8 Tropical Mango Spring Rolls With Avocado-Cilantro Dipping Sauce

You'll have no problem eating your vegetables when these tropical mango spring rolls are what's for lunch.

9 Marinated White Bean Salad

You'll look forward to lunch all day when this marinated white bean salad is packed in your work bag.

10 Chopped Salad With Balsamic-Dijon Vinaigrette

Instead of getting a takeout chopped salad, whip up this homemade take that packs chickpeas, turkey, mozzarella, bulgur, cucumbers, and more into each bite.

11 Apple Cabbage Detox Salad

For a lightened-up take on classic coleslaw, whip up this detox salad. Apples marry with the dynamic duo of red and Savoy cabbage, combining for a fiber-filled meal that aids in digestion. The addition of fennel seeds adds to the fiber factor, plus a kick of cayenne boosts your metabolism.

12 Grilled Chicken, Beet, Apple, and Spinach Salad With Strawberry Vinaigrette

If your ideal salad is a mix of savory and sweet, this grilled beet and apple salad offers the ideal fruity twist on traditional greens.

13 Crunchy Zucchini Noodle Wrap

Add a little healthy crunch to your lunch with these zucchini noodle wraps that have avocado and cheese for maximum flavor.

14 Summer Rolls With Peanut Sauce

Like all Summer rolls, this recipe has the best texture on the day it's made but is also still solid the next day and is a great change-up from your typical lunch routine.

15 Spicy Peanut Soba Noodle Salad

This edamame- and carrot-packed spicy peanut soba noodle salad will keep you full all afternoon long.

16 Copycat Trader Joe's Mexicali Chicken Salad

Make your own Trader Joe's Mexicali Chicken Salad for the whole week instead of spending money on a bunch of them.

17 Spicy Tomato Soup

While your office probably doesn't have a stovetop or oven, chances are there's a functioning microwave. Make a pot of spicy tomato soup and reheat it at work. (Swap crumbled Cheez-Its for the grilled cheese croutons since leftover grilled cheese isn't great.)

18 Black-Bean Salad

Corn kernels, black beans, and confetti-like diced bell peppers, radishes, and red onion combine into a hearty, salsa-like salad.

19 Chickpea, Farro, and Greens Salad

A Greek-inspired salad of chickpeas, farro, greens, olives, and feta will inspire lunch-envy from your co-workers.

20 Slow-Cooker Beans and Quinoa With Scallions, Cheddar, and Hot Sauce

A big batch of slow-cooker beans and quinoa yields enough for a whole week of lunches. (Hot sauce, scallions, and cheddar ensure you won't be sick of it by Friday.)

21 Quinoa Tabbouleh Salad

For a sturdier spin on classic tabbouleh, use quinoa instead of bulgur.

22 Thai Peanut Quinoa Salad

If you like pad thai, then you'll love this Thai-influenced quinoa recipe. It contains all of the best elements of the classic dish, such as peanuts, cilantro, and bean sprouts, with a fun twist: it substitutes quinoa for rice noodles.

23 Raw Gorilla Wraps

Give your cold-cut sandwich routine a rest and opt for this creative lunch instead. These fresh, raw, and fast wraps feature a unique filling: ground walnuts, seasoned with tamari (a Japanese soy sauce that contains little to no wheat), cumin, and other spices and topped with your favorite salsa.

24 Sun-Dried Tomato Pasta Salad

With its piquant flavors and toothsome texture, sun-dried tomato pasta salad is sure to rise you out of a midday slump.

25 Steak Noodle Salad

Adding steak to a heaping pile of greens and noodles is a delicious way to use up leftover beef. Follow this recipe and toss the noodles in the dressing the night before. That way, it's easy to pack the next morning.

26 Veggie and Hummus Sandwich

If you're tired of a vegan-friendly sandwich that doesn't satisfy, prepare to build the best veggie sandwich ever. Hummus and avocado provide the fiber and protein you need to stay fueled, and layers of tasty veggies offer crunch, flavor, and important nutrients your body needs.

27 Quinoa Salad With Cherries and Feta

Make use of cherries when they're in season with a quinoa salad with cherries and feta. The juicy-salty combo mixes with parsley, shallot, and almonds for a brown-bag lunch like you've never tried.

28 Lemon Tuna Salad

Substituting lemon and olive oil for mayonnaise makes for a lighter, brighter tuna salad that's perfectly in line with the Mediterranean diet. Tuck yours into a pita, or serve it on top of a bed of fresh greens.

29 Cucumber Corn Salad

At under 300 calories, one serving of this cucumber corn salad offers almost 12 grams of fiber and 10 grams of protein, so after enjoying one crunchy bite after another, you'll feel full, satisfied, and energized.

30 Cucumber Caprese Salad

Another riff on the classic caprese comes in the form of this hydrating and refreshing cucumber caprese salad that might turn into your new go-to recipe. Higher in protein, fiber, and anti-inflammatory omega-3s, this delicious salad aligns with your healthy goals.

31 Kale, Almond, and Shredded Veggie Salad

This satisfying, refreshing, and detoxifying vegan salad offers over 10 grams of protein and almost 40 percent of your daily recommended fiber. Whether you're starting to feel under the weather or simply want to boost your immune system, this salad should become your go-to lunch, since it offers over 300 percent of recommended vitamin C for the day.

32 Spicy Chicken Chili

Substituting ground chicken for beef in this spicy chili recipe brings the same traditional flavors and textures of the classic you crave — just lightened up for your healthy diet. Make a pot on Sunday night, and pack up a hearty serving for lunch.

33 Burrito Bowl

Move over, Chipotle. This healthy burrito bowl recipe is half the calories and easy to make (the secret is precooked chicken breast). In under 10 minutes, you'll have a fresh lunch that hits all your Mexican takeout cravings. Plenty of protein plus a dollop of Greek yogurt keep bloat at bay.

34 Guacamole Mason Jar Salad

Step away from the chips and guac! This zesty guacamole mason jar salad offers a satisfyingly tangy crunch with every single bite.

35 Avocado-Tuna Salad With Cucumber Noodles

We recommend packing this avocado-tuna salad with cucumber noodles with one major caveat: be polite to your neighbors by not eating it at your desk (unless you have an office to yourself).

36 Spicy Turkey Taco Salad

This spicy turkey taco salad is for those who like a bit of heat in their food.

37 Vegan Bean Salad

After a hard midday workout, this vegan bean salad is the perfect, protein-packed refreshing meal. It has more than 13 grams of fiber and nearly 17 grams of protein to fill you up and help your muscles recover.

38 Slow-Cooker Gigante Beans

39 Smashed Avocado Chickpea Salad

Go vegetarian at lunchtime with this avocado chickpea salad. It comes together in less than 10 minutes and will fill you up until dinner.

40 Chicken Fajita Bowls With Quinoa

Thanks to the flavorful marinade in this recipe for chicken fajita bowls with quinoa, this chicken reheats much more nicely than leftover cooked chicken breasts tend to, making it a good brown-bag lunch option.

41 Slow-Cooker Chili

Low effort to make (it's a straightforward, set-it-and-forget-it slow-cooker recipe) and great as leftovers, this hearty chili is a no-brainer inclusion to this list.

42 Chicken Caesar Kale Mason Jar Salad

Keep your croutons crunchy and your lettuce wilt-free by packing this chicken and kale caesar for lunch at the office.

43 Eggless Kale Caesar Salad

For just the same addictive taste as the original with an added bonus — vitamin-A-rich superfood kale — opt for this lighter Caesar salad dressing instead. This recipe omits the egg yolk and a lot of the cheese for a lower-calorie dressing that tastes exactly like the classic. Using kale instead of romaine lettuce increases the nutrients, adds texture and flavor, and allows the salad to keep in your fridge without going limp.

45 Instant Pot Chicken Noodle Soup

47 Sesame-Ginger Soba Noodle Salad

48 Zucchini Noodle, Quinoa, and Pea Salad

This zoodle, quinoa, pea, and feta salad is so fresh and so green.
