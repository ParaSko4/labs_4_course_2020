Things to do today in Bengaluru

18 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

A gallery of photographs
A new exhibition in town is showcasing around 100 photographs by late Prabuddha Dasgupta. Called Prabuddha Dasgupta: A Journey, it features a large body of his photographic work and comprises his personal work as well as commissioned work. He had worked on numerous commercial projects which mainly involved advertisement campaigns for fashion and lifestyle houses. In spite of them being commercial photographs, he injected a unique and creative spirit into each of them which changed the way this stream of photography was perceived in India and signalled a major breakthrough in the field.

WHERE: NGMA, Palace Road
WHEN: 11am-6.30pm, till January 20
CALL: 22342338Watch a cartoon show

Watch a cartoon show
YS Nanjunda Swamy has created over a thousand cartoons and two hundred caricatures within a short span of four years, and won many accolades and awards in this period. Some of his famous cartoon caricatures depict Donald Trump, Kim Jong-un, Stephen Hawking, Serena Williams, PV Sindhu and Roger Federer. Go, check them out.

WHERE: Indian Cartoon Gallery, Midford Garden Road
WHEN: 10am-6pm, on view till November 23
CALL: 9241658361

25 years of arts
In an effort to encourage emerging voices in the arts and to foster cultural exchange, an exhibition called Continuum has been put together. It’s a selection of art from 25 years of India Foundation for the Arts grant-making programme. On display will be the works of artists such as Alakananda Nag, Soumya Sankar Bose, Anitha Balachandran, Dhruv Jani, Avik Mukhopadhyay, Sarbajit Sen, Pallavi Paul and Sahej Rahal and Abul Kalam Azad, and these include everything from photographs, video projection, animation projection, and gaming projection.

WHERE: Gallery, RMZ Ecoworld, Bellandur WHEN: 11am-6pm, on view till November 28

Check out this collection
Designer label Dev R Nil has stocked its collection of Festive/Resort 2019 at this boutique. The delicately hand-embroidered tissue sarees have detailing that highlight the amount of human hours that have been put into each of them, and the ethos and aesthetic within which they have been conceptualised. The hand-woven tissue sarees are embroidered with muted thread combinations on which minute detailing – pearls, threadwork etc. – are put along the hand-cut scalloped edges.

WHERE: Collage Shop India, Wood Street, Ashok Nagar WHEN: 10am-7.30pm , November 18 CALL: 25566818

Get funny
Mondays are heavy to deal with. We know it. But good for you that, Bangalore Comedy Club has the perfect plan to beat your Monday blues. They have lined up Arnav Rao, Sanjay Manaktala, Rajesh Hinduja, Prasad Bhat and Kritarth Srinivasan for a standup comedy show. So what’s your excuse?

WHERE: The Beer Cafe, 4th Block, Koramangala WHEN: 8.30pm, November 18
ENTRY: `150, insider.in

Pretty them up
The fad for drip cakes is not showing any sign of slowing down. Why will it, when it’s so easy, fun and tasty to make? So put on your aprons and head
to this workshop to learn how to decorate your cakes, make a caramel sauce and create your own Swiss meringue buttercream too. Plus, you will get to learn an upside-down ice cream cone, how to achieve smooth, straight and sharp edges on your cake, and, yes, create very stylish drips on them.

WHERE: Flourish School of Baking and Cake Decor, Brigade Road
WHEN: 3pm-6.30pm, November 18
ENTRY: `4,400, eventshigh.com

Grab your tickets
FIVESIXZERO, Bengaluru’s own dance music fest, is coming up. To be held in association with Bangalore Mirror, the event will explore aspects of space, history, communities, talent, uprisings and more, all through the lens of the music industry at a summit, and then at a big concert. The concert is divided into three categories – the ‘Main Stage’ is dedicated to commercial music and will be headlined by Krewella and Nucleya, ‘De:Vision – The Underground Stage’ will cover the underground side of techno and deep-house and will be headlined by Sal O Cin, and the third one, called ‘Homegrown Stage’, will feature the famous DJs of Bengaluru. The fest is an initiative of Times Red Cell.

WHERE: The Lalit Ashok, Kumarakrupa Road WHEN: November 22-24 TICKETS: bookmyshow.com
DETAILS: fivesixzero.in

Music makers

Simply put, it’s a jamming meetup where musicians assemble and play together. But on some days, they also host workshops and open mic performances.

WHERE: MyBoTree, 3rd Floor, 5th Block, Koramangala
WHEN: 7.30pm-9.30pm, November 18
CALL: 9886419424

With comedy, from Venus

Humans have been exploring the unknown since the time immemorial. Join this journey with a play called The Boy with No Shadow this weekend. The story takes the age-old desire by rulers to wage war and expand boundaries to a new time and space dimension.
It is a comic tale of a peace-loving kingdom on the planet Venus, ruled by an absent-minded king and a very patient queen, both of whom are assisted by a military chief, who is preocuppied with making a magic potion. A little boy passing by, drinks a bottle of one of the potions ordered by the Commander out of curiosity and turns invisible, which leads to comedy of errors and endless entertainment. This is being presented by Indian Cancer Society & Bangalore Little Theatre, which every year tries bring to you a play from different part of the world. So this year, they decided on a different planet altogether – the Venus.

WHERE: Alliance Francaise, Vasanth Nagar WHEN: 7pm onwards, November 22-23

ENTRY: bookmyshow.com
