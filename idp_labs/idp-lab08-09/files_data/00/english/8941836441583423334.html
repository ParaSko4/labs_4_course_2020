Denver radio station says host wasn’t fired over Trump views

18 Nov 2019

DENVER (AP) - A Denver radio station says one of its hosts wasn’t fired in the middle of his show over criticism of President Donald Trump.

Craig Silverman said he was fired by news and talk radio station KNUS-AM Saturday during a segment about former Trump personal attorney Roy Cohn that included critical remarks about the president.

But station general manager Brian Taylor said at a press conference Sunday evening that the show was interrupted because Silverman appeared on a competing radio station and announced that on his KNUS program Saturday.

Taylor says Silverman is an independent contractor and wasn’t fired. He said Silverman’s contract remains in effect.

Silverman, a lawyer and former Denver prosecutor, told KMGH-TV that his contract doesn’t prohibit him from appearing on other media outlets.
