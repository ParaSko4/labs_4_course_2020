First winter-like storm to erase record heat

18 Nov 2019

INLAND EMPIRE - (INT) - Sundays record high temperatures will be only a faint memory by the end of the week.

A brief outburst of Santa Ana winds ignited thermometers at many locations. Riversides 90 erased a record that had stood for 70 years. Heat records were also broken in San Jacinto, Idyllwild, Thermal and Los Angeles. Palm Springs 92 tied a record for the date. Corona was the Inland Empires hot spot at 93. Anaheim, Santa Ana and Escondido shared Southland highs of 94.

A drastic change is coming that will normalize the regions November weather.

- What: Rain, mountain snow (starting at 8,000 ft Tuesday night and lowering to 6,000 ft Wednesday night), mountain fog, and gusty winds in the mountains and deserts.

Low risk of flash flooding at this time.

Preliminary precipitation amounts:

 Coastal mountain slopes: 1.0-1.75 inches
 Orange County and the Inland Empire: 0.50-1.00 inches
 High Desert: 0.25-0.50 inches
 Lower Desert: 0.10-0.25 inches

Several inches of snow possible. Preliminary forecast amounts:

 6000-7000 FT: 2-5 inches
 7000 FT+: 6-10 inches

- Where: All of Southern California.

- When: Precipitation increases area wide Tuesday night with the brunt occurring late Tuesday night through Thursday morning.

The National Weather Service said Monday the scenario in this storm track often produces heaviest precipitation east and south of LA and northern Orange County. Areas from Big Bear to Riverside County mountains to much of San Diego County are expected to have highest totals.

Looking further ahead, high pressure returns for the weekend with slowly warming temperatures.
