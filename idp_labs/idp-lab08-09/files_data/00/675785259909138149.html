Strictly fans baffled over 'best bits' change to results show as Michelle exits

Strictly Come Dancing fans were left baffled over short clip of Michelle Visage's 'best bits' - something that had never happened before

18 Nov 2019, 00:02 by Brogan-Leigh Hurst

Strictly Come Dancing fans were left puzzled by the new feature added on tonight's results show which saw Michelle Visage leave.

When the Strictly hosts announced Michelle, 51, would be the latest celeb to depart from the show, a flashback of her best memories was shown.

However it hadn't been done in previous episodes, so fans were left confused over the new move.

One Twitter user asked: "How come Michelle got a best bits? No one else has?"

Another said: "Wait, do they usually do best bits?"

And a third added: "Why is Michelle getting 'best bits' they haven't done that for others?"

Michelle became the eighth celebrity to leave Strictly Come Dancing, leaving her co-stars stunned.

 

The Drag Race star was previously hailed as the potential winner of the BBC dancing competition, so her exit came as a shock to many viewers.

She and Giovanni Pernice landed in the bottom two with YouTuber Saffron Barker and AJ Pritchard, meaning both couples had to dance again.

Michelle and Giovanni performed their couples choice dance, which was street commercial, to Madonna's iconic Vogue.

 

Meanwhile Saffron and AJ Quickstepped to Beverley Knight's Marvellous Party in a bid to impress the judges.

Craig Revel Horwood deliberated over the decision, admitting that he found it difficult to pick who to save.

Eventually, however, he said: "I'm having a really difficult time with myself over this. This doesn’t normally happen to me. I normally make a very quick, instant decision. This has been really, really difficult.

"One couple took all the notes on board and one couple were better. And the couple I've decided I would like to save is save is Saffron and AJ."

Motsi Mabuse also decided to save Saffron and AJ after saying that they had seen "two brilliant dances".

Finally, Bruno Tonioli agreed, saying that Saffron and AJ's dance was "on the money".
