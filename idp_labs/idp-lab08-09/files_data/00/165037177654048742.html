Barca go ahead of Real Madrid on the 'Forbes' revenue list

18 Nov 2019 by BeSoccer

Barcelona have received big news from the magazine Forbes and the list that is produced every year by the consulting firm 'Deloitte'. For the first time, they are the club with the most revenue in the world and have surpassed Real Madrid in 2019.

The data of all the biggest clubs in the world has been put on the table. In particular, there are four teams that always fight for the top spot in the magazine 'Forbes', with regards to the level of revenue. 

Florentino Perez's Real Madrid have almost always occupied the top spot in recent years, but in 2019 there is an exception. The work of the Barcelona board of directors and its impact throughout the world has grown in such a way that it has been reflected in the data of the consulting firm 'Deloitte'.

For the first time, Barcelona have become the club with the most revenue in the world, with a total of 852 million euros, which is 162 more than in 2018, when they had 690. The Catalan club budgeted 1000 million euros in their last assembly.

For their part, Real Madrid remain in second place with an income of 757 million euros, as they only had six million more than the previous year, as reported by 'AS'.

However, there are aspects that Real Madrid are the best in, without a doubt. In the EBITDA, which is the amount of profits before taxes, interest, etc., and in net debt, the white club is far ahead of Barcelona.

In the first of these indicators, the Real Madrid has 124 million euros for the 72 of Barcelona. As for the second, the team directed by Bartomeu has a debt of 217 million for the 24 of Real Madrid.

The other two clubs behind Real Madrid and Barcelona are Manchester United, which has an income of 732 million, and Bayern Munich, which achieved 652 million.
