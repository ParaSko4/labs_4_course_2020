Spain v Romania declared high risk

18 Nov 2019 by BeSoccer

The Spanish government has declared the Spain v Romania Euro 2020 qualification match high risk.

A large crowd is expected for the final Euro 2020 match. With the Wanda as a host, España play Romania in practically a dead rubber match, as Romania are in the playoffs.

However, although there can be a party in the stands, the Spanish government has declared the match high risk, so there will be a greater police presence, according to 'AS'.

Around 1000 members of the security forces will ensure fans' safety both inside and outside the stadium, as well as hundreds of health personnel.

The Romanian community is the 2nd largest foreign population in Spain. Almost 700,000 people of Romanian origin live in Spain and 700 are expected to have got tickets for the match.
